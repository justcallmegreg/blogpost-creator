Turning "Make It Sound Good" Into Rules a Machine Can Follow

Ask an AI to "make it sound good" and you tend to get the same flat, generic text back. The reason is simple: words like "professional" and "tasteful" don't actually constrain anything, so the model falls back to the average.

The fix is almost dull — replace the adjectives with rules you can check. Not "tasteful emojis" but "0–2 emojis." Not "be professional" but "no hype words." A vague vibe becomes a short checklist you can verify line by line, and it generalizes to any prompt where "good" is the whole requirement.
